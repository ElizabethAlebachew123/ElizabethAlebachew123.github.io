Online shops that portray unrealistic body types:


Fashion Nova: This is a popular online shop with A lot of celebraties supporting them. An issue that pops up a lot is that the cloths where made for people with Hourglass bodies. This can be discouraging and can make people feel insecure with there bodies. The models on Fashion Nova have these Ideal "Perfect Hourglass" bodies With thigh gaps and a slim waist which excludes many groups of people and can be discouraging espacially because Fashio Nova is whats "in"right now. Alle Connell from the Revelist tried on popular Fashion Nova cloths and said,"If I tried this on as a younger woman, I would hate myself. I would think this was how clothing was meant to fit, and that my body was the problem." This is what a lot of young girls may be feeling as well.





This is Aerie, they are a body positive brand! Aerie makes it
their mission to promote self-love, from selling a wide range
of sizes, to partnering with the National Eating Disorders
Association (NEDA). Not only does Aerie feature curvy models,
they also have ethnically diverse ones. Additionally, thanks to
their new support in the fitting room program, customers can
stick encouraging notes on the mirrors. You can use the
#AerieReal hashtag to share untouched photos of yourself!
