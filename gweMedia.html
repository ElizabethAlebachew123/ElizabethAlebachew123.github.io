*Disclaimer: The purpose of this site is to educate and bring awareness to the impact of social media on body image. We want others to accept all body types, including influencer body types. However, we also want women and girls to be aware that there are many different body sizes and shapes that are all beautiful and that conforming to one body type is impossible.




Unrealistic Bodies we see on Instagram

Whenever we scroll through Instagram we see these "perfect bodies". Impossibly curvy yet, impossibly slim and it makes us wonder why don't I look like them? It's hard not to feel this way when society has changed the way social media is used to portray ourselves. When we see Instagram being flooded with so called "influencers", it changes the way we see actual influencers. An influencer is a user with established credibility, large following, and endorsements. They are portrayed to be ideal, and when they're compared to someone on Instagram who wouldn't be defined as real, it can change the way a lot of people, especially young girls, feel about themselves and the world around them. In return, this can lead to people going to extreme measures, physically and mentally, just to reach the level of satisfaction they "need" from society.



Famous body positive influencers!:


This is Ashley Graham. She is a well-known model in the industry for
those who classify as curvy models. She is not afraid of calling out
various modeling industries who exclude certain groups of people and has
been an inspiration for many young girls. Also, she is a large advocate for
body features, such as stretch marks, to become more normalized in our
society. In 2016, she starred on the cover of the famous swimwear magazine,
Sports Illustrated, in hope that the cover would inspire other women to love
their own body, no matter what shape or size. Furthermore, Ashley says that,
"I think there are too many women who are self-conscious about the way
they look, the way they see themselves in the mirror. I hope that this cover is
a testament to them, proving to them that they are beautiful no matter what."




This is Megan Rose Lane. She is a beauty blogger, entrepreneur, and mental
health advocate who shares her struggles with confidence and body image after
she gave birth. By telling her empowering stories through social media, she hopes
to build confidence in other young women. Megan uses her platforms to inform
and support those who have gone through the same experiences, such as eating
disorders and mental health issues. Megan says that, "I realise that this world of
ego, comparison, shallow materialistic insufficiency is suffocating me and deep
down I KNOW happiness cannot be bought, earned or worked toward. Happiness
is not 10lbs from now, it's not when you get your dream job, or when you're prettier,
richer or more popular. Happiness is NOW. So stop fearing what isn't real and decide
to live fully in the moment, fully in love with who you are on a level that's so much
deeper than your eyebrows, achievements or shoe collection." We LOVE that and we
encourage you to go followe her for more empowering quotes!
